# Reject Rejection

**Reject the rejection. Keep the dignity.**

Reject Rejection is a playful AI-powered web tool designed for job seekers who are tired of cold, vague, and overly templated rejection emails.

Instead of silently absorbing another “after careful consideration” message, users can paste a rejection email, choose what happened in the hiring process, select a tone, and generate a witty but risk-aware reply draft.

The product is not built to encourage harmful behavior. It is designed as an emotional support tool for candidates: a way to process frustration, regain a sense of control, and optionally turn anger into a more professional response.

Live demo: [Reject Rejection](https://reject-rejection.netlify.app/)

---

## Product Overview

Job searching can be emotionally exhausting, especially when candidates invest time in applications, interviews, case studies, or unpaid assignments and receive only generic rejection emails in return.

Most job-search tools focus on helping candidates improve their CVs, cover letters, or interview performance. Reject Rejection explores a different product angle:

> What happens after the rejection?

Reject Rejection helps users transform rejection emails into humorous, reflective, or professionally pointed reply drafts.

---

## Core Value Proposition

Reject Rejection gives job seekers three things:

1. **Emotional relief**  
   Users can turn frustration into humor instead of internalizing every rejection.

2. **Controlled expression**  
   The tool generates drafts, but never sends emails automatically.

3. **Candidate experience awareness**  
   It highlights how poor hiring communication affects candidates and employer branding.

---

## Target Users

This product is designed for:

- Job seekers applying to many roles
- Junior candidates facing repeated rejection
- Career switchers
- Candidates who completed interviews or assignments without meaningful feedback
- People who want to process rejection with humor rather than self-blame
- Users who want either a professional response or a “therapy draft” they will never send

---

## Key Features

### 1. Rejection Email Input

Users can paste a rejection email into the tool.

The product is designed around common rejection patterns such as:

- “Unfortunately…”
- “After careful consideration…”
- “We have decided to move forward with other candidates…”
- “We will keep your profile on file…”

---

### 2. Hiring Process Context

Users can select what happened before the rejection:

- Just applied
- Had interviews
- Did an assignment
- Got ghosted
- No idea

This allows the generated response to better match the emotional and professional context.

---

### 3. Tone Selection

Users can choose different reply styles, such as:

- Professional
- Polite but pointed
- Corporate mirror
- More savage
- LinkedIn-style reflection

The goal is to give users both emotional and practical options.

---

### 4. Risk-Scored Reply Drafts

The product concept includes risk awareness.

Some drafts may be safe and professional enough to send. Others are intended only for emotional release.

The core product principle is:

> Funny enough to heal, safe enough not to ruin your career.

---

### 5. User Control

Reject Rejection does not automatically send emails.

Users can:

- Copy the generated draft
- Download it as a `.txt` file
- Make it more professional
- Make it more savage
- Turn it into a LinkedIn post
- Regenerate the draft
- Save it to the Rage Archive

---

### 6. Rage Archive

The Rage Archive allows users to save generated drafts for themselves.

This feature supports the emotional use case of the product: sometimes the user does not actually want to send anything — they just want to feel heard.

---

## Product Philosophy

Reject Rejection is built around a simple insight:

> Candidates are not disposable inbox items.

The product is intentionally humorous, but the underlying problem is serious. Hiring processes often create emotional damage through poor communication, vague feedback, ghosting, and unpaid assignments.

Reject Rejection gives candidates a small moment of agency.

---

## Product Principles

### 1. Vent first, send carefully

The product allows users to generate emotionally satisfying drafts, but sending is always a separate user decision.

### 2. Critique the process, not the person

The tool is designed to criticize poor hiring communication and vague recruitment processes, not to attack individual HR professionals.

### 3. Humor over harassment

The tone can be sarcastic or playful, but it should avoid threats, personal attacks, discrimination, or abusive language.

### 4. Candidate dignity matters

The tool exists to help users feel less powerless during the job search process.
